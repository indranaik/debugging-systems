# Systems Thinking

Learning systems is more important than learning tools.

Tools change constantly, but systems principles remain the same.

Strong production engineers are evaluated by:
- reasoning under pressure
- isolating bottlenecks
- identifying failure domains
- prioritizing incidents
- reducing customer impact
- understanding distributed systems

## Common Production Debugging Questions

- All APIs are slow, where do you start?
- How do you confirm it is a real issue?
- What metrics do you check first?
- How do you identify a bad node?
- What causes high latency with normal CPU?
- What is p95 vs p99 latency?
- Why is average latency misleading?
- How do you debug intermittent latency?
- When do you rollback?
- When do you scale vs restart?

## What Interviewers Evaluate

Interviewers evaluate:
- systems thinking
- debugging methodology
- prioritization
- communication
- operational maturity
- mitigation-first mindset

## Final Takeaway

The best engineers are not the ones who memorize tools.

They are the ones who can:
- stay calm during outages
- reason systematically
- validate hypotheses
- reduce customer impact
- learn from failures
